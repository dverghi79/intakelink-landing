The problem

You sent the intake checklist 2 weeks ago. You're still missing 4 documents and the client thinks they're done. And they think they're done.

Your onboarding process starts with a checklist email. The client uploads 12 files, 3 of which are duplicates and 2 are the wrong version. You email back asking for corrections. They respond a week later with the same files renamed. Meanwhile, the engagement start date keeps slipping and your team is billing hours on follow-up instead of real work.

01

Your current intake is a patchwork of email, Word doc, and a Clio matter you open manually. When a prospective client reaches out after a consultation, you are sending three separate follow-ups: a questionnaire, an engagement letter, and a LawPay link. Some respond to one. Rarely all three.

02

For a solo immigration attorney, each new client intake takes 2-4 hours of non-billable admin time. Multiply that across 12-15 new cases a month and you are spending two full working days doing paperwork that a paralegal at a larger firm handles. You do not have a paralegal.

03

You spent years mastering immigration law so you can help people stay, reunite, and build a life here. You spend your evenings chasing incomplete intake forms and unsigned engagement letters instead. The intake should take care of itself.

How it works

You send one link. Your client completes the intake. The retainer arrives signed.

Step 1

Get complete, correct submissions the first time

IntakeLink generates a single intake URL you can copy into any email or calendar invite. Your client clicks it and fills out their immigration details — no account required, no PDF downloads, no back-and-forth.

Step 2

Stop spending hours following up on missing documents

When the client submits their intake form, IntakeLink prepopulates your engagement letter template and sends it for e-signature in the same session. No manual merge, no separate DocuSign login.

Step 3

Start cases faster, without delays

The signed engagement letter triggers a LawPay payment request for your retainer amount. Your client pays. You get a notification. The matter is funded and ready to open in Clio.
