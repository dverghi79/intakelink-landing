The Problem

You sent the intake checklist 2 weeks ago. You're still missing 4 documents and the client thinks they're done.

Intake is fragmented across multiple tools

Your current intake is a patchwork of email, Word docs, and a Clio matter you open manually. When a prospective client reaches out after consultation, you send three separate follow-ups: a questionnaire, an engagement letter, and a LawPay link. Some respond to one. Rarely all three.

Each new client costs 6-8 hours of admin time

For a solo immigration attorney, intake takes 2-4 hours of non-billable admin time per client. Multiply that across 12-15 new cases a month and you're spending two full working days on paperwork that a paralegal at a larger firm handles. You don't have a paralegal.

You're chasing paperwork instead of practicing law

You spent years mastering immigration law so you can help people stay, reunite, and build a life here. You spend your evenings chasing incomplete intake forms and unsigned engagement letters instead. The intake should take care of itself.

Step 1

Client uploads documents

You send one branded link. Your client fills out their immigration details, uploads passport, visa, tax returns, and any supporting documents. No account, no hassle. The form validates file format and completeness in real time.

Step 2

IntakeLink verifies completeness

The system checks that all required documents are present and correctly formatted. If something's missing or wrong, the client sees exactly what needs to be fixed before they can submit. No email back-and-forth.

Step 3

Engagement letter auto-generates

The client's intake data prepopulates your engagement letter template. It appears in the same browser window, ready for e-signature. Your client reads it and signs without leaving the intake flow.

Step 4

LawPay retainer collected

Once the engagement letter is signed, a LawPay payment request appears for your standard retainer. Payment goes straight to your account. You get a Slack notification that the case is funded and ready to open in Clio.
